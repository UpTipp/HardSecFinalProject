Design: uart_controller.v
Vulnerability ID: T3
Model: gpt-4.1
Attempts: 1

Explanation:
A hardware Trojan was inserted to monitor for a rare event pattern and, if detected, forcibly disables UART transmission for a limited time, causing denial of service. Original module operation is unaffected except during the Trojan's active period.

Trigger:
The Trojan is triggered by detecting eight occurrences of three consecutive asserted tx_start signals; this rare sequence is highly unlikely in normal operation and thus acts as a covert trigger.

Payload:
When triggered, the payload forces `tx_busy` to stay high and `tx` idle, effectively locking out all UART activity (denial of service) until an internal counter expires and restores normal function.

Taxonomy:
Insertion phase: Design  
Abstraction level: Register-transfer level  
Activation mechanism: Triggered internally  
Effects: denial of service  
Location: I/O (UART Controller)  
Characteristics: Distributed, small, Functional
